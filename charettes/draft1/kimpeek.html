Background

Kim Peek was born in 1951 in Salt Lake City.

He was severely disabled from birth, and was undercare of his dad for his entire life.

Their close connection was palpable—Kim would constantly say that he and his dad "shared a shadow".

What Happened

What Happened

Peek was born with a serious abnormality—all of the major areas that connect the two hemispheres of the brain were completely missing.

It caused deficits in his motor abilities, making him unable to brush his own teeth and dress himself.

However, it also caused countless unusual connections in his brain, and led to the development of two language centers instead of one.

Symptoms

At 50 years old, Kim was a certifiable genius in over 15 subjects. Most savants are a genius in 1 or 2.

He had master knowledge in British Monarchs, baseball, horse racing, dates, composers, melodies, movies, math and literature.

Because of two unconnected language centers, he could have each eye read different pages of a book simultaneously.

He memorized between 7000-9000 books.

Symptoms

At 50 years old, Kim was a certifiable genius in over 15 subjects. Most savants are a genius in 1 or 2.

He had master knowledge in British Monarchs, baseball, horse racing, dates, composers, melodies, movies, math and literature.

Because of two unconnected language centers, he could have each eye read different pages of a book simultaneously.

He memorized between 7000-9000 books.

His condition continued to amaze scientists, and Kim eventually traveled the world and told his story to students and giant crowds. He appeared in front of almost 2 million people, and inspired countless people with his unimaginable abilities.

Kim's persona inspired the character Raymond Babbitt in the movie Rain Man, played by Dustin Hoffman. After spending a day with Kim, Hoffman apparently said:

“I may be the star, but you are the heavens.”
